Fighting clutter is like fighting weeds—the writer is always slightly behind. New varieties sprout overnight，and by noon they are part of American speech. Consider what President Nixon’s aide John Dean accomplished in just one day of testimony on television during the Watergate hearings. The next day everyone in America was saying 「at this point in time」 instead of 「now.」

与杂乱斗争就像与杂草斗争 —— 作者总是稍微落后一些。新品种在一夜之间萌芽，到了中午，它们就成了美国语言的一部分。想想尼克松总统的助手约翰 · 迪恩在水门事件听证会上仅仅一天的电视作证就取得了怎样的成就。第二天，美国的每个人都在说「此时此刻」而不是「现在」。

Consider all the prepositions that are draped onto verbs that don’t need any help. We no longer head committees. We head them up. We don’t face problems anymore. We face up to them when we can free up a few minutes. A small detail，you may say—not worth bothering about. It is worth bothering about. Writing improves in direct ratio to the number of things we can keep out of it that shouldn’t be there. 「Up」 in 「free up」 should-n’t be there. Examine every word you put on paper. You’ll find a surprising number that don’t serve any purpose.

考虑一下所有用在不需要任何帮助人们就能领会的动词上的那些无用的介词。我们不再担任委员会主席。我们领导他们。我们不再面对问题了。当我们能腾出几分钟的时间时，我们就会勇敢地面对他们。一个小细节，你可能会说 ーー 不值得费心。这值得为之费心。写作能力的提高与我们能够避免的那些不应该存在的事情的数量成正比。「free Up」中的「Up」应该不在那里。检查你写在纸上的每一个字。你会发现没有任何意义的词数量惊人。

Take the adjective 「personal,」 as in 「a personal friend of mine,」 「his personal feeling」 or 「her personal physician.」 It’s typical of hundreds of words that can be eliminated. The personal friend has come into the language to distinguish him or her from the business friend，thereby debasing both language and friendship. Someone’s feeling is that person’s personal feel.ing—that’s what 「his」 means. As for the personal physician，that’s the man or woman summoned to the dressing room of a stricken actress so she won’t have to be treated by the impersonal physician assigned to the theater. Someday I’d like to see that person identified as 「her doctor.」 Physicians are physicians，friends are friends. The rest is clutter.

比如形容词「personal」 ，比如「a personal friend of mine」 ，「his personal feeling」或「her personal physicist」 这是数百个可以被删除的单词中的典型。私人朋友已经进入语言区分他或她从商业朋友，从而贬低语言和友谊。一个人的感觉就是那个人的个人感觉ーー这就是「他的」的意思。至于私人医生，就是那些被召唤到受伤女演员更衣室的男人或女人，这样她就不必接受分配到剧院的非个人医生的治疗。总有一天我希望看到那个人被确认为「her doctor」。医生就是医生，朋友就是朋友。其余的都是杂物。

Clutter is the laborious phrase that has pushed out the short word that means the same thing. Even before John Dean，people and businesses had stopped saying 「now.」 They were saying 「currently」 (「all our operators are currently busy」)，or 「at the present time,」 or 「presently」 (which means 「soon」). Yet the idea can always be expressed by 「now」 to mean the immediate moment (「Now I can see him」)，or by 「today」 to mean the historical present (「Today prices are high」)，or simply by the verb 「to be」 (「It is raining」). There’s no need to say，「At the present time we are experiencing precipitation.」

杂乱是一个费力的短语，它挤出了表达同样意思的短词。甚至在约翰•迪恩之前，人们和企业已经不再说「now」 他们说的是「currently」(「all our operators are currently busy」) ，或者「at the present time」 ，或者「presently」(意思是「很快」)。然而，这个概念总是可以用「now」来表示的(「Now I can see him」) ，或者用「today」来表示日期上的现在(「Today prices are high」) ，或者简单地用动词「to be」(「It is raining」)来表示下雨。没有必要说，「At the present time we are experiencing precipitation.」

「Experiencing」 is one of the ultimate clutterers. Even your dentist will ask if you are experiencing any pain. If he had his own kid in the chair he would say，「Does it hurt?」 He would，in short，be himself. By using a more pompous phrase in his professional role he not only sounds more important; he blunts the painful edge of truth. It’s the language of the flight attendant demonstrating the oxygen mask that will drop down if the plane should run out of air. 「In the unlikely possibility that the aircraft should experience such an eventuality,」 she begins—a phrase so oxygen-depriving in itself that we are prepared for any disaster.

「Experiencing」是其中一个最终的离合器。甚至你的牙医也会问你是否「experiencing any pain」。如果他有自己的孩子坐在椅子上，他会说，「Does it hurt? 」 简而言之，他说自己觉得自然的话。而通过在他的职业角色中使用更加华而不实的词语，他不仅让自己听起来更加重要，而且还模糊化了痛苦真相的边缘。这是美国空中乘务员航空管理局演示氧气面罩的语言，如果飞机的空气耗尽，氧气面罩会掉下来。「在不太可能发生的情况下，飞机会遇到这种情况」她这样开头ーー这个短语本身就是如此缺氧，以至于我们已经为任何灾难做好了准备。

Clutter is the ponderous euphemism that turns a slum into a depressed socioeconomic area，garbage collectors into waste-disposal personnel and the town dump into the volume reduction unit. I think of Bill Mauldin’s cartoon of two hoboes riding a freight car. One of them says，「I started as a simple bum，but now I’m hard-core unemployed.」 Clutter is political correctness gone amok. I saw an ad for a boys’ camp designed to provide 「individual attention for the minimally exceptional.」

混乱是沉闷的一种委婉说法，它把「贫民窟」变成了「经济萧条的社会经济地区」，把「拾荒者」变成了「废物处理人员」，把「城镇垃圾场」变成了「减排单元」。我想起了比尔·莫尔丁的卡通画，画中两个流浪汉骑着一辆货车。其中一个人说: 「我开始只是个普通的流浪汉，但现在我已经失业了」。凌乱的政治正确变得疯狂起来。我看到一个男孩训练营的广告，旨在为「最低限度的特殊人群提供个人关注」。

Clutter is the official language used by corporations to hide their mistakes. When the Digital Equipment Corporation eliminated 3,000 jobs its statement didn’t mention layoffs; those were 「involuntary methodologies.」 When an Air Force missile crashed，it 「impacted with the ground prematurely.」 When General Motors had a plant shutdown，that was a 「volume-related production-schedule adjustment.」 Companies that go belly-up have 「a negative cash-flow position.」

杂乱是企业用来隐藏自己错误的官方语言。当数字设备公司(Digital Equipment Corporation)裁员3000人时，它的声明并没有提到裁员：公司只是采用了「非自愿的方法」。当一枚空军导弹坠毁时，它「过早地与地面一起撞击」。当通用汽车公司关闭了工厂，这是一个「与产量相关的生产时间表调整」。破产的公司说自己有了「负的现金流状况」。

Clutter is the language of the Pentagon calling an invasion a 「reinforced protective reaction strike」 and justifying its vast budgets on the need for 「counterforce deterrence.」 As George Orwell pointed out in 「Politics and the English Language,」 an essay written in 1946 but often cited during the Vietnam and Cambodia years of Presidents Johnson and Nixon，「political speech and writing are largely the defense of the indefensible…Thus political language has to consist largely of euphemism，question-begging and sheer cloudy vagueness.」 Orwell’s warning that clutter is not just a nuisance but a deadly tool has come true in the recent decades of American military adventurism in Southeast Asia and other parts of the world.

杂乱就是五角大楼把入侵称为「强化保护性反击」，并以「反力量威慑」的需要为其巨额预算辩护时说的语言。正如乔治 · 奥威尔(George Orwell)在《政治与英语》(Politics and the English Language)一文中指出的那样，「政治演讲和写作在很大程度上是为站不住脚的东西辩护... 因此，政治语言必须在很大程度上包括委婉语、请求提问以及完全模糊的含糊语言。」这篇文章写于1946年，但在约翰逊和尼克松做总统的年代被经常提及。奥威尔警告说，近几十年来，美国在东南亚和世界其他地区进行军事冒险，杂乱不仅是一种麻烦，而且是一种致命的工具。

Verbal camouflage reached new heights during General Alexander Haig’s tenure as President Reagan’s secretary of state. Before Haig nobody had thought of saying 「at this juncture of maturation」 to mean 「now.」 He told the American people that terrorism could be fought with 「meaningful sanctionary teeth」 and that intermediate nuclear missiles were 「at the vortex of cruciality.」 As for any worries that the public might harbor，his message was 「leave it to Al,」 though what he actually said was: 「We must push this to a lower decibel of public fixation. I don’t think there’s much of a learning curve to be achieved in this area of content.」

在亚历山大·黑格将军担任里根总统的国务卿期间，口头伪装达到了新的高度。在黑格之前，没有人想到「在这个成熟的时刻」指的是「现在」 他告诉美国人民，可以用「有意义的制裁牙齿」来打击恐怖主义，中程核导弹「处于关键性的漩涡中」 至于公众对他可能隐藏了什么的担忧，他的回应是「交给艾尔处理」 ，尽管他实际上说的是: 「我们必须把这个问题推到公众关注的最低分贝。我不认为在这个内容领域需要多少学习曲线。」

I could go on quoting examples from various fields—every profession has its growing arsenal of jargon to throw dust in the eyes of the populace. But the list would be tedious. The point of raising it now is to serve notice that clutter is the enemy. Beware，then，of the long word that’s no better than the short word: 「assistance」 (help)，「numerous」 (many)，「facilitate」 (ease)，「individual」 (man or woman)，「remainder」 (rest)，「initial」 (first)，「implement」 (do)，「sufficient」 (enough)，「attempt」 (try)，「referred to as」 (called) and hundreds more. Beware of all the slippery new fad words: paradigm and parameter，prioritize and potentialize. They are all weeds that will smother what you write. Don’t dialogue with someone you can talk to. Don’t interface with anybody.

我可以继续引用各个领域的例子 ーー 每个行业都有越来越多的专业术语，用来给民众泼冷水。但是这个清单会很乏味。现在提出这个问题的意义在于提醒人们，杂乱是我们的敌人。那么，请注意那些比短词好不了多少的长词: 「assistance」(帮助)、「many」(许多)、「convenient」(简易)、「individual」(男人或女人)、「remainder」(休息)、「initial」(首先)、「implement」(do)、「enough」(足够)、「attempt」(尝试)、「referred to as」(调用)等等。小心所有滑溜溜的时尚新词: 范式和参数，优先排序和潜力。它们都是会窒息你所写的东西的杂草。如果你要用这些词，不要和你可以交谈的人对话。不要和任何人打交道。

Just as insidious are all the word clusters with which we explain how we propose to go about our explaining: 「I might add,」 「It should be pointed out,」 「It is interesting to note.」 If you might add，add it. If it should be pointed out，point it out. If it is interesting to note，make it interesting; are we not all stupefied by what follows when someone says，「This will interest you」? Don’t inflate what needs no inflating: 「with the possible exception of」 (except)，「due to the fact that」 (because)，「he totally lacked the ability to」 (he couldn’t)，「until such time as」 (until)，「for the purpose of」 (for).

同样阴险的还有所有词组，我们用它们来解释我们打算如何进行解释: 「我可能会补充说，」「应该指出来，」「值得注意。」 如果您可以添加，请添加它。如果应该指出来，就指出来。如果有趣的话，记录下来，让它变得有趣; 当有人说「这会让你感兴趣」的时候，我们是不是都被这句话弄糊涂了？ 不要膨胀不需要膨胀的东西: 「除了可能的例外」(except) ，「由于」(because) ，「他完全没有能力」(他不能) ，「直到」(until) ，「为了」(for)的目的。

Is there any way to recognize clutter at a glance? Here’s a device my students at Yale found helpful. I would put brackets around every component in a piece of writing that wasn’t doing useful work. Often just one word got bracketed: the unnecessary preposition appended to a verb (「order up」)，or the adverb that carries the same meaning as the verb (「smile happily」)，or the adjective that states a known fact (「tall skyscraper」). Often my brackets surrounded the little qualifiers that weaken any sentence they inhabit (「a bit,」 「sort of)，or phrases like 「in a sense,」 which don’t mean anything. Sometimes my brackets surrounded an entire sentence—the one that essentially repeats what the previous sentence said，or that says something readers don’t need to know or can figure out for themselves. Most first drafts can be cut by 50 percent without losing any information or los.ing the authors voice.

有没有办法一眼就认出杂物？ 这是我在耶鲁的学生发现的一个有用的装置。我会在一篇不起作用的文章的每个部分都加上括号。通常只有一个词被括起来: 动词(「order up」)后面附加了不必要的介词，或者与动词(「smile happily」)意思相同的副词，或者表示已知事实的形容词(「tall skyscraper」)。我的括号通常包含一些小的限定词，这些限定词削弱了他们所使用的任何句子(「一点」、「某种程度上」) ，或者像「在某种意义上」这样的短语，它们没有任何意义。有时候，我的括号会包围整个句子 —— 基本上是重复前面的句子，或者说读者不需要知道或者自己就能够理解的句子。大多数初稿可以被删减50% 而不会丢失任何信息或失去作者的声音。

My reason for bracketing the students’ superfluous words，instead of crossing them out，was to avoid violating their sacred prose. I wanted to leave the sentence intact for them to analyze. I was saying，「I may be wrong，but I think this can be deleted and the meaning won’t be affected. But you decide. Read the sentence without the bracketed material and see if it works.」 In the early weeks of the term I handed back papers that were festooned with brackets. Entire paragraphs were bracketed. But soon the students learned to put mental brackets around their own clutter，and by the end of the term their papers were almost clean. Today many of those students are professional writers，and they tell me，「I still see your brackets—they’re following me through life.」

我把学生们多余的单词划掉，而不是删掉，是为了避免侵犯他们神圣的文章。我想让他们完整地分析这个句子。我说，「我可能错了，但是我认为这个可以被删除，这个意思不会受到影响。但是你决定。阅读没有括号内容的句子，看看是否有用。」 在学期的最初几个星期，我把挂着括号的文件退回去。整个段落都被括起来了。但是很快，学生们就学会了在他们自己的杂物周围加上心理括号，到学期结束时，他们的论文几乎是干净的。今天，这些学生中有许多是职业作家，他们告诉我，「我仍然能看到你的括号ーー他们一直跟随着我。」

You can develop the same eye. Look for the clutter in your writing and prune it ruthlessly. Be grateful for everything you can throw away. Reexamine each sentence you put on paper. Is every word doing new work? Can any thought be expressed with more economy? Is anything pompous or pretentious or faddish? Are you hanging on to something useless just because you think it’s beautiful?

你可以培养同样的眼睛。找出你写作中的混乱，然后无情地删除它。对你可以扔掉的一切心存感激。重新检查你写在纸上的每一句话。每个词都有新意吗？ 所有的思想能以更经济的方式表达吗？ 有没有什么是华而不实或者自命不凡或者时髦的？ 你是不是仅仅因为觉得某样东西很漂亮就紧紧抓住它不放？

Simplify，simplify.

保持简洁，保持简洁。
